I'm Chi-Ning Chou, currently a research assistant under the supervision of Dr. Kai-Min Chung at Institute of Information Science (IIS), Academia Sinica. I received my Bachelor degree in Computer Science and Information Engineering at National Taiwan University this (2016) June with minor in Mathematics.

I love theoretical computer science (TCS) and plan to persue a Ph.D. in TCS. Specifically, I'm interested in the field of complexity theory, algorithm analysis, combinatorics, optimization, and high-dimensional statistics. I wrote a book about computational complexity in Chinese and conducted several research in TCS and statistics (see my research page). Currently I'm working on investigating the computational power of spiking neural networks.

Outside academics, I love playing baseball and rubiks cube. I also enjoy listening to classical music, especially Beethoven's piano sonata. I maintain a blog with some of my thought and posts about what I've learned.
